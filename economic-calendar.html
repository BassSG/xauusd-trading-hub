Gold Price Sensitivity Matrix

How different events typically affect gold prices

📉 Stronger USD / Higher Rates
Typically Bearish for gold. Higher rates increase opportunity cost of holding non-yielding gold.
📈 Weaker USD / Lower Rates
Typically Bullish for gold. Lower rates reduce opportunity cost and USD weakness support gold.
🛡️ Geopolitical Risk
Bullish for gold. Safe-haven demand surges during uncertainty and global tensions.
📊 Risk-On (Strong Equities)
Typically Bearish for gold. Capital flows to higher-yielding assets during risk appetite.
💰 Inflation Surprise
Bullish for gold. Gold is traditionally an inflation hedge; unexpected inflation drives prices higher.
🏦 Central Bank Tightening
Bearish for gold. Aggressive policy tightening strengthens USD and raises real interest rates.
